Deviant 3D

By: Calvin Wong, Rafael Ostrea,
Sambodh Mitra, Chris Park

Summary

Deviant3D is a 3D, multiplayer shooter game utilizing a wide variety of the graphical tools and skills learned over the course of CS 184. The base platform utilized to create Deviant3D is Godot, an open-source game engine. Topics such as geometry processing, lighting, animation, and physical simulation were all employed to create the world, characters, and varying interactable objects.

A wide variety of obstacles were also encountered. Initially, three of our team members were completely unfamiliar with Godot, and thus needed to learn how to create via a game engine from scratch. In addition, several technical complications also arose, such as Godot's inbuilt physics engine not supporting multiplayer play. These issues were usually remedied by either creating new systems in-house, such as development of a deterministic physics system, or reading the resources linked at the bottom of the website.

Our final result was a polished game that successfully allowed for cohesive multiplayer gameplay. The interactable objects included varying enemy types, powerups to aid combat, as well as procedurally generated terrain. Many stylistic improvements were also added over the course of development, including, but not limited to, character and world shaders, improved unit models, as well as varying field-of-views offering both third-person as well as first-person perspectives.

Technical Details

Deviant3D was created using Godot, an open-source game engine, which made collision testing and basic rendering trivial, but we also encountered a variety of problems as well, such as Godot's inbuilt physics being nondeterministic, forcing development of a deterministic physics system.

Creation of world + procedural generation Creation of characters/enemies + how gravity works Creation of 3D movement + Hooke’s Law Creation of Powerups Camera shake using Simplex Noise Lots of other simplex noise applications, terrain generation, water shader EXplain non-linear tweening/interpolation Blend Trees
