Deviant 3D

By: Calvin Wong, Rafael Ostrea,
Sambodh Mitra, Chris Park

Summary

Deviant3D is a 3D, multiplayer shooter game incorporating a wide variety of graphical tools and skills learned throughout CS 184. Topics such as geometry processing, lighting, animation, and physical simulation brought the world, characters, and varying interactable objects to life.

Our final result was a polished game with cohesive multiplayer gameplay. The interactable objects included varying enemy types, powerups to aid combat, as well as procedurally generated terrain. Many stylistic improvements were also added during development, including, but not limited to, character and world shaders, improved models, as well the ability to toggle between third-person and first-person cameras.

Technical Details

Deviant3D was created using Godot, an open-source game engine, which made collision testing and basic rendering trivial, but we also encountered a variety of problems as well, such as Godot's inbuilt physics being nondeterministic, forcing development of a deterministic physics system.
